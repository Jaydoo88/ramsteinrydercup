First Annual Ramstein Ryder Cup
Official Rules, Draft Procedures & Competition Format

Event Dates: September 16–20
Competition Days: September 17–19
Location: Scottsdale, Arizona
Golf Course: Troon North Golf Club
Commissioner: Jason Dousharm
Event Organizer: Daylene Koch

1. Event Purpose and Spirit
The Ramstein Ryder Cup is a team golf competition created to build an annual tradition centered on:
• Competitive but fair play
• Team camaraderie
• Sportsmanship
• Pace of play
• Clear and consistent rules
This is the First Annual Ramstein Ryder Cup. All players are expected to support both the competition and the long-term success of the event.

2. Event Leadership and Authority
Event Leadership
• Commissioner: Jason Dousharm
• Event Organizer: Daylene Koch

Commissioner Authority
The Commissioner has final authority on:
• Player tier classification (A / B / C / D)
• Official rules interpretation
• Final rulings when captains cannot agree
• Final approval of the official rules packet

Event Organizer Role
The Event Organizer supports:
• Event logistics and coordination
• Rules distribution and communication
• Scorecard and standings collection
• Welcome Day and awards presentation logistics
• Ruling coordination support

On-Course Decision Rule
• Each group is responsible for making good-faith rulings in real time
• If a dispute cannot be resolved, play continues and the issue is noted
• Final rulings are handled after the hole or round
• If the Commissioner is involved in a match, they should not act as the sole decision-maker

4. Teams and Player Tiers
Each team will consist of 4 golfers:
• 1 A Player
• 1 B Player
• 1 C Player
• 1 D Player
Total: 4 players per team

Women’s Teams
• Two women’s teams (Team A and Team B) will be drafted on Welcome Day
• Women participate in the Day 1 and Day 2 bonus-point competition

5. Player Tier Classification
Player tiers are assigned by the Commissioner prior to the draft.
Factors may include:
• Skill level
• Recent play
• Consistency
• Competitive balance
Once the draft begins, classifications are final.

6. Captains and Responsibilities
Captains are responsible for:
• Draft participation
• Setting pairings
• Verifying scores
• Managing pace of play
• Resolving minor rules issues
Captains may not change core rules during play.

7. Draft Procedure
• Captains are announced
• Draft order is determined
• Players are drafted
• Women’s teams are drafted
• Teams are finalized before competition begins

8. Competition Format and Total Points
Each competition day:
• Day 1: 2 matches (36 points total)
• Day 2: 2 matches (36 points total)
• Day 3: 3 matches (54 points total)

Event total:
• Day 1: 36 points
• Day 2: 36 points
• Day 3: 54 points

Total competition points:
• 126 points

Additional:
• Women’s matches award 5 bonus points per day (Day 1 and Day 2 only)
• Bonus points are awarded to the men’s team aligned with the winning women’s team

Winning Team:
• Team with the most total points wins the Ramstein Ryder Cup

9. Daily Match Formats
Day 1 – 2-Man Scramble (Match Play)
• AB vs AB
• CD vs CD

Day 2 – 2-Man Scramble (Match Play)
• AD vs AD
• BC vs BC

Day 3 – Couples Scramble (Final Day Format)
• 3 matches’ total
• 18 points per match
• Pairings determined by captains
• Couples must remain on the same team
• Extra golfers are assigned to balance teams

10. Pairings and Fairness Rules
• No repeat partners
• Pairings rotate by tier:
Day 1: AB / CD
Day 2: AD / BC
Day 3: AC / BD (if possible)
• Each team includes A, B, C, and D players
• Matchups must be like-for-like
• Any exceptions require captain and Commissioner approval

11. Hole-by-Hole Scoring
• Win hole: 1 point
• Tie: 0.5 points
• Loss: 0 points

Each match totals 18 points.

12. Scorekeeping
• Men’s matches opposing team keep score
• Women’s matches (Day 1 and Day 2): each team keeps their own scrambled score
• Scores must be tracked hole-by-hole
• Final scores must be verified by both sides

13. Women’s Competition Format
• Two women’s teams (Team A and Team B)
• 3-player scramble format
• Played on Day 1 and Day 2
• Winning team earns 5 bonus points
• Bonus points are awarded to the aligned men’s team
• Women’s teams switch sides on Day 2

14. Out of Bounds (OB) Rule
• OB is treated as a lateral drop
• 2-stroke penalty applies
• Ball placed near exit point
• No closer to the hole
• Groups make quick decisions to maintain pace

15. Relief Rules
• No special relief system is used
• Players may take relief within one club length
• Relief must not be closer to the hole

16. Tees, Mulligans, and Gimmies
• All players use designated tees
• No mulligans
• No automatic gimmies
• Putts must be holed unless conceded
• Tap-ins may be conceded within the leather

17. Pace of Play
• Keep up with the group ahead
• Be ready to hit
• Limit delays
• Ball search limit: 3 minutes
• Make quick rulings and continue playing

18. Disputes and Rulings
• Attempt to resolve within group
• If unresolved, note and continue
• Commissioner reviews after round
• Scores are final once next hole begins

19. Sportsmanship
• Respect all players and staff
• No cheating or intentional mis-scoring
• Competitive but respectful environment

20. Tiebreaker
If tied after all competition points are completed:
1) Most points won on Day 3
2) Back 9 score
3) Back 6
4) Back 3
5) 18th hole
6) Co-champions or agreed playoff

21. Weather and Adjustments
Captains and Commissioner may:
• Delay play
• Shorten rounds
• Adjust scoring
• Modify format if necessary

22. Absences and Substitutions
• Pairings may be adjusted
• Substitutes allowed with approval
• Replacement players assigned fair tier

23. Awards
• Winning team receives the Ramstein Ryder Cup
• Trophy awarded after Day 3
• Traveling trophy held by winning captain

24. Future Updates
• No mid-round rule changes
• Adjustments made after event
• This serves as the baseline for future events
